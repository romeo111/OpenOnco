Red Flag Origin

DefinitionAdvanced/metastatic RCC patient with ECOG PS 2 or significant comorbidities reducing tolerability of high-intensity ICI+TKI combinations (lenvatinib+pembrolizumab in particular). ECOG 2 = ambulatory and capable of self-care but unable to carry out any work activities; up and about >50% of waking hours. Lenvatinib+pembrolizumab (CLEAR trial) has higher rates of G3-4 toxicity than nivolumab+ipilimumab or pembrolizumab+axitinib — ECOG 2 increases risk of early dose modification / treatment discontinuation and shifts the benefit-risk ratio toward less intense TKI backbone (cabozantinib, axitinib) or ICI-doublet.

Notes

In the CLEAR trial, lenvatinib+pembrolizumab had the highest ORR (~71%) but also the highest G3-4 toxicity (71.8%) and dose modification rate (68.8%) of the 1L regimens. ECOG 2 patients were included (PS 0-2) but post-hoc tolerability data suggest they experience disproportionate toxicity. Preferred alternatives for ECOG 2: pembro+axitinib (KEYNOTE-426 had ECOG 0-2; well-tolerated) or nivo+ipi (for IMDC intermediate/poor, ECOG 0-1 per CM-214 eligibility — but observed up to ECOG 2 in compassionate use). Cabozantinib-based combos (nivo+cabo) also acceptable. Engine should down-weight lenv+pembro when ECOG 2 fires.
